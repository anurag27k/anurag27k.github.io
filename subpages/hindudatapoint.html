  COLLEGE PROJECT  


Hindu data point Visualization

As a part of a course we were asked to redesign a Hindu data point.






01

Project Brief

In this project we were asked to select a Hindu Data Point (Hindu article with statistical data) and redesign it to make better sense or easy perception of it. It contains vizualizations showing how the prices for petrol have increased in Delhi over the year and how it compares with that in other cities and countries.

Data point chosen: Petrol price peaks in India owing to high taxes and crude oil prices
Link: Hindu Data point


02

Analysis




Figure: Price of petrol in some cities

In the chart, X-axis plot shows the timeline and the Y-axis plot shows the selling price of petrol. Initially the author tries to compare the prices between the major cities. Probably the author, for this article, has chosen to show only the metropolitan cities. The data is shown from the dates Jun 16 2017 to May 30 2021 (approx. 4 years).

Over here we there is a huge dip in the prices between approximately years 2019-20. We also see a sudden rise of the graph from late 2020. The reason behind these two phenomena is unknown. In other areas of the graph we see a steady increase in the prices of petrol.

Approximating the entire country's data only on the metropolitan cities is a bit absurd. X-axis should contain more values to make more sense of the data. The choice of colors for the graph also seems to be random choice.


Figure: Crude price vs pump price

The chart depicts the petrol price in Delhi (left axis) against the crude price - Indian basket (right axis) between January 2019 and May 2021.

Here the author wants to showcase the price of crude oil and the selling price of petrol. The Orange line shows that the prices of crude oil and the red line shows the prices of selling petrol. We see a huge dip in the price of crude oil during april (which was mentioned in the article). Surprisingly the selling petrol price was majorly increasing and saw a small a tiny dip. This majorly hints us at the increases taxes during this period. When the crude oil price was back up in the graph, the taxes continued to rise.

In the first glance the view is confused seeing the two graphs and two Y-axis. The viewer tends to compare the red graph with the orange graph. These are two graphs merged into one and should not be compared on the same scale. Also there is no index supporting which Y-axis is corresponding to which color. In this graph too the border dates are only shown. We also observe inconsistency in the date range. The first graph shows us data for about 4 years and the second shows for little lesser than 1.5 years. The reason of dip is not mentioned but is mostly due to lockdown.


Figure: Petrol price breakup

In the next picture we see a table where we see the spiltup of the retail price of petrol. Again we see a different duration is seen over here. One striking point is that we see the taxes increase on a huge margin having centre's tax the biggest contributing factor even though the base price of petrol has reduced.


Figure: Comparision of prices with other countries

In this graph we see ordinal values as countries on the X-axis. The chosen countries are said that they have comparable economies but the exact metric for comparison is not provided. The Y-axis tells us about the price per litre in Indian Rupees. The values could have been represented in here. Also South Africa, Russia and Brazil are not neighbours.
